Dental Sealants

A simple, highly effective preventative measure to protect the biting surfaces of your back teeth from decay.

How Dental Sealants Protect Your Teeth

A dental sealant is a tooth-colored acrylic "coating" painted onto the biting surface of the back teeth (molars and premolars).

They effectively "seal" the deep grooves and depressions (called pits and fissures) that are notoriously difficult to clean with a toothbrush, acting as a physical barrier against plaque and harmful acids.

Benefits of Choosing Sealants

  • Maximum Protection: Protects the deep depressions where food particles and plaque accumulate, areas that even diligent brushing and flossing often miss.
  • Quick & Simple: The procedure is non-invasive, painless, and takes only a few minutes to seal each tooth.
  • Durable and Long-Lasting: Sealants hold up extremely well under the force of normal chewing and can last several years before a simple reapplication is needed.

Both children and adults can significantly benefit from dental sealants in the fight against tooth decay.
