The Vital Role of Oral Hygiene

Oral hygiene is essential for eliminating bacteria. If not removed regularly, bacteria builds up to form plaque , which hardens into calculus (tartar) . This process can lead to serious, long-term ailments:

  • Gingivitis: A mild, reversible form of gum disease.
  • Periodontal Disease: A more severe condition leading to inflammation, infection, and potentially tooth loss.

A diligent home care routine combined with regular professional check-ups is the best defense against these complications.

Why Get an Exam?

  • Early Detection: We detect problems like decay and gum disease early, when they are quickest and easiest to fix.
  • Care Evaluation: We evaluate your current dental care methods and provide personalized suggestions for improvement.

Frequency

Dental exams are typically recommended every six months .

However, we may suggest more frequent visits based on factors such as:

  • Smoking status
  • Frequency of cavities
  • Gum disease (periodontal health)
  • Genetic susceptibility to decay

What to Expect

  • Professional Cleaning: Our hygienist will thoroughly clean your teeth, removing plaque and hardened calculus (tartar) using professional instruments.
  • Comprehensive Check: We will check for signs of decay, gum disease, and perform a vital oral cancer screening .
  • Diagnostic Imaging: An X-ray may be performed to provide a detailed summary of your oral health and identify any hidden issues beneath the surface.
  • Personalized Guidance: We will review your current habits (brushing, flossing, fluoride use) and demonstrate proper techniques to promote optimal oral health.

How to Prepare

The best preparation is practicing good home care: brushing twice a day and flossing once a day . Additionally, please be ready to share:

  • Your full medical and dental history.
  • Current insurance coverage details.
  • Honest information about your daily oral health habits.
