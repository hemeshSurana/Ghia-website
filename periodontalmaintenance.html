Periodontal Maintenance (Gum Disease)

Understanding Gum Disease

Periodontal diseases are infections of the gums that gradually destroy the supporting bone and tissue of your natural teeth. This damage is primarily caused by dental plaque , a sticky, colorless film that constantly forms on your teeth.

If plaque is not carefully removed by daily brushing and flossing, it hardens into a rough, porous substance known as calculus (or tartar) . Plaque bacteria release toxins that irritate the gums, causing them to turn red, swell, and bleed easily—the first stage, Gingivitis .

[Image of Gingivitis to Periodontitis progression]

Periodontal Disease Progression

As the irritation is prolonged, the gums separate from the teeth, creating pockets (spaces) .

If left untreated, the supporting gum tissue and bone deteriorate, leading directly to tooth loss . In fact, adults over 35 lose more teeth to gum diseases than from cavities.

Accelerating Factors

While plaque is the primary cause, the disease can be accelerated by several key factors:

  • Smoking
  • Diabetes
  • Stress
  • Clenching and grinding teeth (Bruxism)
  • Certain Medications
  • Poor Nutrition

Preventing Gum Disease

The best defense is effective daily brushing and flossing , combined with regular professional examinations and cleanings .

If periodontal disease starts, professional intervention is necessary to prevent its progress and save your teeth. Please consult with our team for a personalized maintenance schedule.
