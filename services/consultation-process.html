What to expect from a periodontal consultation

A periodontal consultation is a comprehensive evaluation by a periodontist who specializes in the diagnosis, treatment, and prevention of gum disease and other conditions that affect the supporting structures of the teeth. Here's what you can typically expect from a periodontal consultation:

Medical history review: The periodontist will review your medical history to get a better understanding of your overall health and any conditions or medications that may affect your oral health.

Oral exam: The periodontist will perform a thorough examination of your teeth, gums, and mouth to assess the health of your gums, bone, and other supporting structures.

X-rays: The periodontist may take X-rays or other images of your teeth and jaws to evaluate bone loss, the position of teeth, and other factors that may affect your oral health.

Periodontal probing: The periodontist will use a small probe to measure the depth of the spaces between your teeth and gums, which can indicate the presence of gum disease.

Diagnosis: Based on the results of the examination, X-rays, and other tests, the periodontist will provide a diagnosis and recommend appropriate treatment options.

Treatment plan: The periodontist will work with you to develop a customized treatment plan that is tailored to your specific needs and goals. This may include nonsurgical therapies, such as deep cleaning and scaling, or surgical interventions, such as periodontal surgery or dental implants.

Education and prevention: The periodontist will also provide education on how to maintain good oral hygiene habits and provide advice on how to prevent future periodontal disease.

Overall, a periodontal consultation is an important first step in maintaining good oral health and preventing the progression of periodontal disease. The periodontist will work with you to develop a personalized treatment plan that can help you achieve optimal oral health and maintain a healthy smile for years to come.
