Understanding Denture Rebase

Rebasing is a procedure recommended when the **denture teeth** are still in good condition but the **denture base material** is not. It involves replacing the entire acrylic denture base (the pink material) to ensure a stable denture without needing new teeth.

A rebase is similar to a reline, but more comprehensive: a rebase replaces all the old pink acrylic denture base material, while a reline only replaces the inner lining that contacts the tissue.

Reasons a Rebase May Be Required

  • To repair a severely **broken or damaged denture**.
  • To restore a **weakened pink acrylic denture base** due to age or stress.
  • For the final adaptation or **replacement of an immediate denture** after the healing phase.

Denture Repairs

A variety of circumstances can lead to a fractured or damaged denture, and these issues should be addressed immediately. Repairs restore the appliance close to its original condition.
