Your dentures are a significant **investment** and require consistent care to maintain their performance and protect your health. The proper **fit** of your dentures profoundly affects your underlying oral tissues and overall health. An annual exam is essential to ensure proper denture function and longevity.

Key Components of the Annual Exam

1. Medical and Dental History Update

Updating your **medical and dental history** is critical, as various systemic conditions, medications, or lifestyle changes can affect your gums and jawbone, potentially altering the fit of your dentures.

2. Oral Cavity Examination

A thorough exam helps **detect oral abnormalities**, such as inflammatory conditions or potentially cancerous lesions, allowing for early intervention and treatment.

3. Tissue and Bone Assessment

Ill-fitting dentures often cause adverse **tissue changes** or accelerate **bone resorption**. We assess the health of your supporting tissues and recommend solutions like relining or rebasing for your specific needs.

4. Comfort, Fit, and Integrity of Dentures

Issues with **bite, speech, and tissue irritation** must be corrected. Dr. Ghia assesses your denture for structural flaws (like cracks or looseness) and ensures the optimum stability and comfort.

Denture Exam Overview: What to Expect

  • Update of medical and dental history
  • Complete oral cavity examination
  • Tissue and bone assessment
  • Denture stability and bite exam
  • Professional **cleaning and polishing** of dentures
  • Review of personal **oral hygiene** practices
